The local authority are only willing to cover up to a fixed rate per hour HRLA.

However the agency charges a higher hourly rate HRAG

The difference between the rates has to be covered by the client (HRAG - HRLA).

Also any hours beyond what was agreed by the local authority has to be covered in full by the client.

The agency provides invoices and timesheets from which the payment calculations can be made.

Spreadsheet

To calculate the payments above a spreadsheet was created. Through time it evolved into the following sub-sheets:

  • Invoice - Data entry of the invoice supplied by the agency:
  1. Account Reference
  2. Invoice Date
  3. Invoice Number
  4. Summary list of timesheets grouped by Week Ending dates from which hours and cost are determined.
  • Timesheets - Copy of hardcopy timesheets recording hours and signed by carer and client:
  1. Timesheet Number
  2. Care Worker Name
  3. List of hours allocated and booked lead by date entry.

  • InvoiceCalc: An auto generated sheet that works out daily hours when given an invoice. Taking into account the hours allocated by the local authority and calculated extra hours.

The main requirements of the spreadsheet

  • Check invoice against recorded timesheets.
  • Determine local authority contributions to invoice.
  • Determine client contributions to be made towards invoice.

Database

The aim is to have a database that can replace the spreadsheet and provide flexibility to cover new requirements (e.g. adding a different agency, extended to work with carers hired directly, produce reports on cost and hours).

Initial thoughts was to replicate the sheets within database tables but experience has shown that the limitations of a spreadsheet should not be applied to a database . . .

So I have started to put together a series of database structures then apply the current and future requirements to test them.
